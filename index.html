About

This repository gathers a set of Jupyter notebooks exploring the interplay between quantum noise, ground-state computation, and machine learning techniques in quantum systems. The project investigates how quantum hardware imperfections influence the estimation of molecular ground-state energies, combining ab initio quantum chemistry frameworks (such as PySCF and Qiskit Nature) with neural-network-based representations of quantum states.

Different mapping techniques — including Jordan–Wigner, Bravyi–Kitaev, and parity encodings — are implemented to translate fermionic Hamiltonians into qubit operators. The notebooks also analyze strategies for error mitigation and variational optimization, comparing classical and quantum samplers, and assessing the stability of energy landscapes in noisy environments. Together, these tools provide a flexible platform for testing hybrid quantum-classical algorithms and studying the scalability of quantum simulations for chemical and condensed-matter systems.
